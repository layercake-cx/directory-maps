## 2. Two Types of Personal Data We Handle

It's important to understand that Layercake plays two different roles depending on whose data is involved.

### 2.1 Account and Subscription Data — we are the Data Controller

This is information about you as a Customer or authorised User of Layercake Maps: for example, names, work email addresses, billing details, and login activity. For this data, **Layercake is the data controller** and is responsible for deciding how and why it is processed, in line with this notice.

### 2.2 Map Content Data — you are the Data Controller, we are the Data Processor

Layercake Maps allows Customers to upload their own data into maps and directories — for example, contact details of members, suppliers, or partners, addresses, free-text descriptions, or other information you choose to include ("**Map Content**").

**Map Content is entered by you, not by Layercake.** Where Map Content includes personal data about third parties (such as an individual's name, email address, phone number, or details entered into free-text fields), **you are the data controller for that information, and Layercake acts only as a data processor**, storing and displaying it on your instructions.

This means:

- **It is your responsibility** to have a lawful basis for collecting and uploading that data, and, where required, to obtain the consent or provide the notice needed from the individuals concerned before adding their details to a map.
- **It is your responsibility** to ensure Map Content does not include special category data (e.g. health information) or other sensitive personal data unless you have a specific lawful basis and appropriate safeguards in place.
- Layercake processes Map Content solely to provide the Software to you (hosting, displaying, and enabling search/discovery of your maps) and does not use it for our own purposes.
- You remain responsible for responding to data subject requests (such as access or deletion requests) relating to Map Content, though we will support you in fulfilling these where the data sits within our systems.

We recommend Customers review their own privacy notices and consent practices before uploading any personal data into a map, particularly free-form text fields, which can easily and unintentionally capture personal data.

### 2.3 Messages Sent Through a Map — you are the Data Controller, we are the Data Processor

Layercake Maps allows a visitor to a Customer's map or directory to send a message to a contact listed on it. We store these messages (including the sender's own name, email address, and message content, and the recipient details) in order to deliver and log the communication.

The same principle in Section 2.2 applies: **the Customer operating the map is the data controller for these messages, and Layercake is the processor.** It is the Customer's responsibility to make clear to visitors, via their own privacy notice, that messages sent through their directory will be stored and delivered, and to handle any data subject requests relating to that correspondence. We retain message data only to provide this feature and do not read, use, or share message content for our own purposes.

## 3. What Account Data We Collect

Where we act as controller (see 2.1), we may collect:

- **Identity and contact data**: name, job title, organisation, email address, phone number.
- **Account data**: login credentials, subscription tier, usage history within the app.
- **Billing data**: invoicing details and payment status (card details themselves are handled by our payment processor and are not stored by us).
- **Technical data**: IP address, browser/device type, log data, and cookies (see Section 9).
- **Communications**: correspondence with our support team.
- **Map usage and activity data**: we log activity on published maps — such as views, searches, and marker or listing clicks — to understand engagement and usage trends and to provide Customers with analytics about their own maps. Where this activity is tied to an identifiable visitor (e.g. via IP address or an account), it is personal data, and we act as controller for the purpose of generating aggregated usage analytics, while making map-specific analytics available to the relevant Customer.

## 5. International Data Transfers

We are based in the UK, and we serve Customers globally, including in Switzerland, the EU, and elsewhere.

- **UK Customers**: your data is processed in accordance with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.
- **EU Customers**: where you are located in the European Economic Area, we process personal data in accordance with the EU GDPR. The UK benefits from an EU adequacy decision, meaning data can generally flow between the UK and EEA without additional safeguards.
- **Swiss Customers**: Switzerland's Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP) applies alongside this notice where relevant. The UK also benefits from an adequacy recognition from Switzerland, supporting transfers between Switzerland and the UK.
- Where we or our sub-processors transfer personal data outside the UK, EEA, or Switzerland (for example, to hosting providers in the US), we put appropriate safeguards in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or reliance on an adequacy decision.

## 6. Sub-processors and Third Parties

We use a small number of trusted service providers to run Layercake Maps. These providers only process data on our instructions and under contractual data protection terms:

- **Hosting and infrastructure** (e.g. Vercel, Supabase) — to host the application and store account and Map Content data.
- **Email delivery** (Resend, using Amazon SES infrastructure) — to send transactional emails (e.g. sign-up confirmation, password resets).
- **Mapping services** (Google Maps Platform) — to render maps and geocode addresses entered as Map Content.
- **Payment processing** — to handle billing (card details are not stored by us).

We do not sell personal data, and we do not share Account Data or Map Content with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

## 7. Data Retention

- **Account Data** is retained for as long as your subscription is active, and for a reasonable period afterwards to meet legal, accounting, or dispute-resolution needs.
- **Map Content** is retained for as long as your subscription is active. Following termination, we make your data available for export for 30 days, after which it may be deleted, in line with our Terms and Conditions.
- **Map usage and activity logs** are retained for as long as reasonably needed to provide trend analytics (typically no longer than 24 months on a rolling basis), after which they are deleted or aggregated so individuals are no longer identifiable.
- **Messages sent through a directory** are retained for as long as the relevant map and Customer account are active, and are deleted along with the associated Customer account on termination in line with Section 2.3. There is not currently a feature allowing Customers to delete individual messages ahead of this.

## 10. Your Rights

Depending on where you are based, you may have rights under the UK GDPR, EU GDPR, or Swiss FADP, including the right to:

- Access the personal data we hold about you.
- Request correction of inaccurate data.
- Request deletion of your data, subject to legal or contractual retention requirements.
- Object to or restrict certain processing.
- Request a copy of your data in a portable format.
- Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.

To exercise these rights in relation to Account Data, contact us using the details in Section 12. If your request relates to Map Content about you (i.e. you believe your details have been entered into someone else's map), please also contact the organisation that manages that map directly, as they are the data controller for that content.
